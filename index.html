About

We live in a very diverse world where everyone is different and unique however one thing every single person can agree on and find common ground is the fact we all procrastinate. We all find it hard to study or complete assignments because writing notes off a piece of paper or memorising content is simply boring. I believe individuals would procrastinate less if the content they studied was presented in a different format and platform. The idea of studying even demotivates some individuals. The study app will aid in the presentation of our study material in the form of a game. The game app that I aim to develop will greatly aid individuals in several circumstances whether it be a quick study session or a long all-nighter for a 3-hour exam. The game will include not only contain components such as a memory game and practise quizzes that are completed in levels but also take into account the learning style of the individual. All the user will need to do is place all the information into the ‘access menu’ and the app will compose it. The study game app will not only be interesting but benefit all individuals as somewhere in our lives we will need to learn some sort of content that doesn’t naturally spark our interest. For example, a student studying for their least favourite subject or an employee needing to learn their new business policies. The fact that the app takes into account the learning style of the individual makes it wholly useful and counteracts that ‘one size fits all’ ideology that is evident in most apps these days. This app will mostly benefit students as it encourages active learning. The main reason behind students failing classes these days may be due to the demotivation that is provoked by the idea of sitting down and learning or memorising content off a book they will probably forget right after they exit the exam. A 2014 study, published by the scientific journal PNAS, found that students in classes with traditional lecturing were 1.5 times more likely to fail than students in environments with active learning. Using the study game app will allow students to not only regularly learn their content but also revisit the content so they are able to revisit this information and remember it. The study game is a very useful app however requires intense user interaction. The game app has various features including a component that allows the individual to identify their learning style. The game begins with a small quiz that will identify your learning style involving whether the individual is a visual, auditory, reading/writing, and kinaesthetic. The user will then place the content on the app. For example, a student can upload set of definitions, explanations and examples they find difficult to remember. Once the individual has placed their content in the app, the app will compose the data and match it up with their learning style in a collection of quizzes, flash cards, lists For example, matching up words with pictures of definitions to suit visual learners. The app includes a very thorough and understandable main menu and also contains levels and a main goal whereby Individuals are able to challenge themselves. For example, completing 10 definitions in a day allows an individual to proceed to the next level with more complicated and advanced definitions. The study game app will be found on the app store and game store and can be purchased for free. The study game will also have a feedback section in the main menu to track user satisfaction and continuously improve the app. Developing the study app will require many tools and software such as coding tools such as Eclipse.For example One software that will be useful and is Appery, is a cloud-based mobile app builder .
